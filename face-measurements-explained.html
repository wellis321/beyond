What We Measure & Analyze

Our advanced face analysis technology uses computer vision to examine specific aspects of your skin quality. Learn about the measurements we take and how they help us understand your skin's unique needs.

Face Detection & Location

Before analyzing your skin, our system first detects and locates your face in the image. This ensures accurate measurements by focusing on the facial area.

Confidence Score Measures how confident the system is that a face is detected (0-100%). We require a minimum 70% confidence before capturing.
Bounding Box Determines the exact position and size of your face in the image (x, y coordinates, width, and height).
Face Landmarks When active, identifies key facial feature points to ensure proper face positioning and alignment.

Skin Quality Analysis

Using OpenCV.js computer vision technology, we analyze your captured image in three key dimensions to assess your skin's condition and provide personalized recommendations.

Smoothness

Measures the texture and surface irregularities of your skin by analyzing how light reflects and interacts with your skin's surface.

How It's Measured:

  • Converts the image to grayscale for texture analysis
  • Applies Gaussian blur to create a smooth reference
  • Calculates the difference between original and blurred image
  • Measures variance - higher variance indicates rougher texture
  • Score: 100 minus (variance × 2) = smoothness percentage

What it reveals: Surface roughness, fine lines, wrinkles, and skin texture quality. Higher scores indicate smoother, more refined skin texture.

Evenness

Evaluates skin tone uniformity and color distribution across your face to identify areas of hyperpigmentation or uneven skin tone.

How It's Measured:

  • Converts image to LAB color space (designed for human perception)
  • Extracts the L channel (lightness/brightness)
  • Calculates standard deviation of lightness values
  • Lower standard deviation = more uniform skin tone
  • Score: 100 minus (standard deviation × 3) = evenness percentage

What it reveals: Color consistency, hyperpigmentation, sun damage, and overall skin tone uniformity. Higher scores indicate more even coloration.

Clarity

Detects blemishes, dark spots, and skin impurities to assess overall skin clarity and health, identifying areas that need attention.

How It's Measured:

  • Converts image to HSV color space (Hue, Saturation, Value)
  • Extracts the S channel (saturation)
  • Applies threshold to detect dark spots and blemishes
  • Counts the ratio of blemish pixels to total pixels
  • Score: 100 minus (blemish ratio × 500) = clarity percentage

What it reveals: Presence of blemishes, dark spots, acne marks, and skin impurities. Higher scores indicate clearer, blemish-free skin.

Color Space Analysis

Our analysis uses multiple color space conversions to extract different types of information from your image. Each color space reveals different aspects of your skin:

RGB

The original color format captured by your camera. Red, Green, and Blue channels represent the base image data.

Grayscale

Used for texture analysis. Converts color information to brightness values, allowing us to focus on surface texture and irregularities.

HSV

Hue, Saturation, Value. The Saturation channel helps identify blemishes and dark spots by detecting areas with different color intensity.

LAB

Designed to match human color perception. The L (lightness) channel provides the most accurate measurement of skin tone uniformity.

Overall Score Calculation

Your overall skin quality score is calculated as the average of all three measurements:

Overall Score = (Smoothness + Evenness + Clarity) ÷ 3

This balanced approach ensures that all aspects of skin quality are considered equally, giving you a comprehensive view of your skin's condition.
